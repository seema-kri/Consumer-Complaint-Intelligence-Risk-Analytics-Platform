
# SQL Insights Report

## Consumer Complaint Intelligence Platform

### Project Overview

This analysis examines 62,516 consumer complaints across financial products and services between 2017 and 2023. The objective is to identify high-risk products, major customer pain points, geographic complaint concentrations, and operational performance indicators.

---

### Executive Insight

The organization demonstrates strong operational performance with a 93.77% timely response rate. However, complaint volume remains heavily concentrated within a small number of financial products and issue categories.

# Product Analysis

## Complaint Distribution by Product

| Product                     | Complaints |  Share |
| --------------------------- | ---------: | -----: |
| Checking or Savings Account |     24,814 | 39.69% |
| Credit Card or Prepaid Card |     16,197 | 25.91% |
| Credit Reporting Services   |      7,710 | 12.33% |
| Mortgage                    |      6,601 | 10.56% |
| Money Transfer Services     |      3,453 |  5.52% |
| Debt Collection             |      2,736 |  4.38% |

### Key Findings

* Nearly 40% of all complaints originate from Checking and Savings Accounts.
* The top two product categories account for approximately 66% of total complaints.
* Deposit accounts and card products represent the largest operational risk areas.

### Business Recommendation

Prioritize customer experience improvements in account management and card services since these products drive most customer dissatisfaction.

# Channel Analysis

| Referral    |     10,766 |
| Phone       |      4,684 |
| Postal Mail |      1,318 |

### Key Findings

* Approximately 73% of complaints are submitted through digital channels.

### Business Recommendation

Continue investing in digital complaint management systems and self-service customer support capabilities.

---

# Strategic Recommendations

1. Prioritize improvements in account management processes.
2. Enhance fraud detection and monitoring for money transfer services.
3. Improve credit reporting accuracy and dispute resolution workflows.
4. Monitor California and other high-volume states through dedicated regional dashboards.
5. Increase operational readiness during Q2 and Q3 peak complaint periods.
6. Strengthen digital customer support channels, which handle most complaint submissions.
7. Focus risk management efforts on Checking Accounts and Credit Card products, which generate nearly two-thirds of all complaints.

---

# Conclusion

The analysis reveals that Checking and Savings Accounts, Credit Card Products, and Credit Reporting Services are the primary drivers of consumer complaints. Account management issues, transaction disputes, inaccurate reporting information, and fraud-related concerns represent the most significant customer pain points. While complaint handling performance remains strong, targeted improvements in these high-risk areas can substantially enhance customer satisfaction and reduce future complaint volumes.
